For this assignment, I utilized Canva to create a one-page infographic that serves as a quick-reference guide for my batchmates. My design primarily focuses on defining digital literacy, highlighting safe internet practices, and outlining basic email etiquette. I divided the visual into three distinct, color-coded sections to make the information easily digestible without overwhelming the reader. The top section introduces the core concept of digital literacy—emphasizing that it is more than just knowing how to use technology, but rather using it responsibly. The middle section provides actionable safety tips like enabling two-factor authentication, while the bottom section offers quick reminders for professional email communication.

One thing I found particularly challenging while making this was striking the right balance between text and imagery. It was tempting to include a lot of detailed information, but I had to actively condense my points into punchy, scannable bullet points. This constraint was interesting, as it taught me how to communicate essential digital skills purely through concise visual design.
